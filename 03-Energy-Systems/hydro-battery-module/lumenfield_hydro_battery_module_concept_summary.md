# LUMENFIELD Hydro Battery Module

**Conceptual Infrastructure Summary (Carbon-First Framing)**

---

## 1. Purpose and Positioning

The LUMENFIELD **Hydro Battery Module** is a gravity-based infrastructure component designed to **recover otherwise-wasted hydraulic energy** from controlled water movement. Its primary value is **carbon offset through energy recovery**, with secondary benefits including grid demand reduction, resilience, and support for low-power applications such as slow EV charging.

This module is **not an energy generation system**. It is an **energy recovery and storage system** embedded in required or unavoidable water movement. Gravity, not continuous pumping, is the dominant operating force.

Carbon offset is the primary objective; all other uses are downstream beneficiaries.

---

## 2. Core Design Principles

- Gravity-first, pump-second operation
- Energy recovery, not generation
- Distributed small tanks rather than large static storage
- Modular replication over bespoke civil works
- Calm hydraulics (low velocity, low turbulence)
- Deterministic, inspectable, infrastructure-grade behavior

---

## 3. Physical Architecture

### 3.1 Containerized Vertical Modules

- Standard containers buried **vertically**
- Provide human-accessible working space
- Structural shell for plumbing, tanks, and inspection
- Each container supports **1–2 vertical gravity loops**

Modules are repeatable and scalable; performance increases by replication, not enlargement.

---

### 3.2 External Vertical Gravity Loops

- Loops are **external** to buildings or domes
- Fully vertical geometry
- Arranged like rolled sheets / scrolls (“rolling paper” form)
- No continuous sealed siphons

Each loop consists of:
- A controlled vertical drop
- A small reset / settling tank with a free surface
- Calm discharge into the next loop or return path

**Golden rule:** Every vertical drop terminates in a tank with a free surface to reset pressure.

---

### 3.3 Small Staggered Tanks

- Individual tank volume: **5–10 m³**
- Atmospheric / vented
- Used for pressure reset, settling, and control
- High turnover (multiple cycles per day)

These tanks are **hydraulic control points**, not bulk storage.

---

### 3.4 Hanging Bladder (Top Reference)

- Small atmospheric hanging bladder at the top of the module
- Typical volume: **1–3 m³**
- Establishes a stable hydraulic reference head
- Decouples pump recharge from gravity-driven operation

The bladder defines where gravity begins; it does not store meaningful energy.

---

## 4. Geometry and Head

### 4.1 Loop Geometry

- Typical vertical drop per loop: **≈ 6 m**
- Optional additional loop above grade: **≈ 5 m**

### 4.2 Reference Configuration

- 4 below-grade loops @ ~6 m each = **24 m head**
- +1 above-grade loop @ ~5 m = **~29 m total head**

Below-grade depth is treated as equal to above-grade height; gravity does not distinguish.

---

## 5. Hydraulics

- Primary pipe size: **DN200**
- Target flow velocity: **≤ 1.5 m/s**
- Typical daily cycled volume: **300–360 m³/day**

Hydraulics are intentionally calm to reduce losses, wear, and maintenance.

---

## 6. Energy Performance (Metric)

### 6.1 Energy Recovery

At ~29 m total head (5-loop configuration):

- Recoverable energy intensity: **~0.05–0.06 kWh per m³** (after losses)
- Daily energy recovered: **~15–22 kWh/day**

### 6.2 Power Characteristics

- Continuous equivalent power: **~0.6–0.9 kW**
- Instantaneous power during active flow: **~2–4 kW**

The system favors continuous low power with optional short peaks, typically buffered if needed.

---

## 7. Carbon Offset Value (Primary Metric)

Using conservative grid intensity assumptions:

- Annual recovered energy: **~5,500–8,000 kWh/year**
- Carbon intensity assumption: **~0.4 kg CO₂e / kWh**

### Annual Carbon Offset

- **~2–3 tonnes CO₂e per module per year**

This offset is:
- Additional (energy otherwise dissipated)
- Persistent
- Infrastructure-linked
- Non-behavioral

---

## 8. Use Cases (Secondary)

While carbon offset is primary, recovered energy may be applied to:

- Grid demand reduction
- Site resilience
- Lighting and controls
- Slow / continuous EV charging (~1 EV/day per module)

These uses do not alter the carbon-first framing.

---

## 9. Integration with LUMENFIELD Dome A

- Hydro Battery Module is **external** to Dome A
- Interfaces only at the service / utility layer
- No interaction with biological or photonic systems
- Dome A remains fully functional without the module

The Hydro Battery Module improves efficiency and resilience without redefining Dome A’s core role.

---

## 10. Summary

The LUMENFIELD Hydro Battery Module is a **distributed gravity infrastructure unit** that:

- Recovers low-grade hydraulic energy
- Delivers measurable carbon offsets
- Operates deterministically
- Scales through replication
- Avoids chemical batteries and complex machinery

It is positioned as **long-life climate infrastructure**, not experimental energy technology.
