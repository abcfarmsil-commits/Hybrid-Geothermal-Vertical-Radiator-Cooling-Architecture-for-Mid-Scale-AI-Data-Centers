\#\# Field Signal: DIY / Owner-Built Geothermal Can Be Cost-Competitive (Reference)

This architecture assumes geothermal is an \*assist layer\* (load smoothing \+ compressor avoidance hours),  
not a single-point-of-failure cooling backbone.

A useful reference point for constructability and cost perception is the DIY geothermal build documented by  
the YouTube channel “Challenged” (residential scale). While not design-basis data for data centers, it is  
directionally useful for:

\- Demonstrating trench-based closed-loop installs are achievable with common excavation equipment  
\- Showing labor \+ excavation dominate cost variance (owner-built vs contractor-built)  
\- Reinforcing why “ground loop as efficiency assist” can pencil for mid-market owners
