## Contact Acquisition Strategy

### Direct Research Methods:
1. **Restaurant Websites**: Most restaurant websites have contact forms or info@ email addresses
2. **LinkedIn**: Connect with chefs and F&B directors directly
3. **Instagram DM**: Many chefs are active and responsive on Instagram
4. **Phone Calls**: Call restaurants during off-hours (2-4pm) and ask for the executive chef's email

### Industry Connections:
1. **Culinary School Networks**: Leverage alumni connections
2. **Food Supplier Introductions**: Ask other suppliers for warm introductions
3. **Chef Referrals**: Once you have one chef, ask for introductions to others
4. **Industry Events**: Attend chef gatherings and exchange contact information

### Contact Management:
1. Create a spreadsheet with the following columns:
   - Restaurant/Business Name
   - Chef/Contact Name
   - Email Address
   - Phone Number
   - Website
   - Instagram Handle
   - Contact Method Used
   - Date Contacted
   - Response
   - Follow-up Date
   - Notes/Interests

2. Prioritize contacts based on:
   - Alignment with your brand
   - Influence in the Chicago food scene
   - Menu style that would benefit from microgreens
   - Personal connections or warm introductions

3. Track all communications meticulously:
   - Date and method of outreach
   - Response received
   - Follow-up actions
   - Specific interests mentioned

## Email Personalization Strategies

### For Fine Dining Chefs:
- Reference specific menu items that could be enhanced with microgreens
- Mention any awards or recognition they've received
- Focus on flavor profile and visual presentation benefits

### For Hotel Restaurants:
- Emphasize consistency and reliability of supply
- Highlight the story and exclusivity for guests
- Focus on year-round availability regardless of season

### For Country Clubs:
- Emphasize member experience enhancement
- Focus on the visual impact for special events
- Highlight the local story and sustainability aspects

### For Casual Innovative Restaurants:
- Focus on affordability and versatility
- Highlight the sustainability story
- Emphasize flavor intensity and nutritional benefits

### For Culinary Educators:
- Focus on educational aspects of microgreens
- Offer demonstration opportunities
- Highlight the growing process and techniques

## Follow-Up Schedule

### Week 1:
- Send initial website introduction email to 10-15 high-priority contacts
- Track opens and clicks

### Week 2:
- Send follow-up emails to non-responders from Week 1
- Send value proposition email to those who opened/clicked
- Add 10-15 new contacts with initial email

### Week 3:
- Schedule sample deliveries for interested contacts
- Send testimonial email to non-responders from Week 2
- Add 10-15 new contacts with initial email

### Week 4:
- Send special offer email to remaining non-responders
- Follow up with sample recipients for feedback
- Begin conversion discussions with interested prospects

### Ongoing:
- Add 10-15 new contacts weekly
- Maintain regular follow-up schedule
- Document all feedback and adjust approach accordingly
