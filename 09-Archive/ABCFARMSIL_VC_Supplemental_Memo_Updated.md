# ABCFarmsIL, INC. – VC Supplemental Memo

## Purpose
This memo supplements the ABCFarmsIL pitch deck and financial model. All statements herein align directly with the submitted financial projections and unit economics.

## Business Model
ABCFarmsIL deploys buried regenerative utility nodes that provide food, soil, water, and energy buffering as civic infrastructure. Core production systems operate underground as sealed, deterministic infrastructure. Surface-adjacent biological modules integrate as optional tenants. Revenue is driven by recurring B2B sales with predictable demand, short sales cycles, and premium pricing tied to freshness and locality.

## Unit Economics (Aligned to Financials)
Each underground infrastructure node is capitalized once and generates recurring weekly revenue. Gross margins improve as labor efficiency increases through standardized workflows. Scaling occurs through replication of modular infrastructure units, not customization, keeping unit economics consistent across sites.

## Workforce Strategy
Workforce partnerships reduce labor acquisition costs without creating operational dependency. Each unit remains profitable at market labor rates, ensuring financial durability independent of subsidies.

## Brand & Infrastructure Partnerships
Brand partnerships provide standardized inputs—energy, equipment, logistics—reducing capex and deployment time. These partnerships do not dilute ownership or compromise operational control. Energy infrastructure partnerships integrate hydro battery modules for carbon offset and resilience.

## Scaling Strategy
Expansion is driven by repeatable unit economics and demand density. Sites are selected for logistics efficiency and customer proximity rather than bespoke build requirements. Underground infrastructure enables deployment in right-of-way, utility easements, and high-density environments unavailable to traditional agriculture.

## Why This Wins
ABCFarmsIL is not a pilot or municipal demonstration. It deploys buried regenerative infrastructure that transforms unused underground space into productive civic utility. This infrastructure-first approach provides margin expansion, speed of deployment, and long-term defensibility by operating in the utility and infrastructure category rather than competing in traditional agricultural markets.
