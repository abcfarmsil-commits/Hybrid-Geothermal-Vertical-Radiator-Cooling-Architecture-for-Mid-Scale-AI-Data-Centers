# Yellow Perch Hatchery Core - 53-ft Reefer Container

**A gravity-first, complete breeding, spawning, and nursery system for Yellow Perch production.**

---

## 🐟 System Overview

This system provides a complete Yellow Perch hatchery within a 53-foot refrigerated container, from broodstock conditioning through early nursery stages. The gravity-first design ensures reliability and reduces energy consumption while maintaining optimal water quality for sensitive life stages.

### Capabilities

- **Maintain multiple brood lines** simultaneously
- **Stage controlled spawns** on demand
- **Hatch and raise** thousands to tens of thousands of fingerlings/year
- **Operate quietly, safely, and predictably**
- **Integrate later** with greenhouse thermal tanks via heat exchanger

---

## 🏗️ System Architecture

### High-Level Flow (Gravity Path)

```
Larvae → Spawn → Brood → Solids Removal → Biofilter → Sump → Clean Lift → Header → Gravity back
```

### Key Design Principles

- **100% gravity flow** through fish → solids → bio → sump
- **Pumps only lift clean water** (low head, isolated)
- **Dedicated zones** for each life stage
- **Redundant systems** for critical operations (air, oxygen)

## 📋 Zone Configuration

### Container Layout (Internal length ~48-49 ft usable)

| Zone | Function | Key Components |
|------|----------|----------------|
| **Zone 2** | Broodstock Conditioning | 2× 400-500 gal tanks, center drain, surface skim |
| **Zone 3** | Spawning | 1-2× 200-300 gal tanks, circular flow, egg-safe drains |
| **Zone 4** | Larval Rearing | 2× 50-100 gal tanks, blacked out, weir overflow |
| **Nursery** | Early Growth | 1-2× 200-300 gal tanks, grading access, bottom drain |
| **Filtration** | Water Treatment | RFS, static screen, MBBR, sump |
| **Utilities** | Support Systems | Pumps, air, oxygen, controls, emergency systems |

---

## 🔄 Water Flow Design

### Gravity-First Philosophy

**Benefits:**
- **Pump-free operation** during normal conditions
- **Fail-safe drainage** if power is lost
- **Energy efficiency** - pumps only for clean water lift
- **Inspector-friendly** - visible, understandable flow

### Flow Sequence

1. **Broodstock tanks** → 4" gravity drain
2. **Spawn tanks** → 3" gravity drain
3. **Larval tanks** → 2" gravity drain with weir overflow
4. **Solids removal** - Radial Flow Settler (RFS)
5. **Biofiltration** - Moving Bed Biofilter (MBBR)
6. **Sump** - Clean water collection
7. **Pump lift** - Only place with pumps (low head, redundant)
8. **Header tank** - Gravity distribution back to systems

## 🐠 Life Stage Systems

### Broodstock Conditioning (Zone 2)

**Equipment:**
- **2× round tanks:** 400-500 gal each
- **Center bottom drain:** 3-4" with screen
- **Sidewall surface skim:** 2" for waste removal
- **Lighting control:** Lids for photoperiod management
- **Temperature control:** Inline heaters staged

**Purpose:** 
- Hold and condition broodstock for spawning
- Maintain optimal health and nutrition
- Control spawning timing through environmental cues

### Spawning System (Zone 3)

**Equipment:**
- **1-2× round tanks:** 200-300 gal each
- **Gentle circular flow:** Egg suspension
- **Egg-safe drains:** Screened to prevent egg loss
- **Optional spawning substrate:** Mats or cylinders

**Purpose:**
- Provide controlled spawning environment
- Protect eggs during fertilization
- Enable egg collection for incubation

### Egg Incubation

**Equipment:**
- **8-16× vertical egg jars** OR tray incubators
- **Gravity-fed manifold:** Needle valves per jar
- **Temperature control:** Precision ±0.5°C
- **Water flow optimization:** Low turbulence

**Purpose:**
- Protect developing eggs
- Optimize hatch rates
- Monitor development stages

### Larval Rearing (Zone 4 - Quiet End)

**Equipment:**
- **2× rectangular/round tanks:** 50-100 gal each
- **Blacked out environment:** Light-sensitive larvae
- **Weir-style overflow:** NOT standpipe (prevents damage)
- **Fine filtration:** Protects delicate larvae
- **Dedicated air supply:** Gentle aeration

**Purpose:**
- Raise larvae through critical first-feeding stage
- Minimize stress and predation
- Optimize survival rates

### Nursery Tanks

**Equipment:**
- **1-2× round tanks:** 200-300 gal each
- **Grading access:** Size-based sorting
- **Bottom drain + overflow:** Efficient cleaning
- **Increased feed capacity:** Higher biomass

**Purpose:**
- Grow fingerlings to stocking size
- Perform health assessments
- Prepare for transfer to grow-out

## 🧪 Water Treatment Systems

### Mechanical Filtration (No Pumps)

**Radial Flow Settler (RFS):**
- **Size:** 150-300 gal
- **Purpose:** Remove perch waste before dissolution
- **Output:** Sludge purge line to drain or tote
- **Gravity-fed:** No energy required

**Static Screen/Microscreen:**
- **Purpose:** Fine solids removal
- **Gravity-fed:** No energy required

### Biological Filtration

**Moving Bed Biofilter (MBBR):**
- **Tank size:** 300-500 gal
- **Media:** K1 or similar, ~40-60% fill
- **Aeration:** Low-pressure air diffuser ring
- **Media retention:** Screen to prevent loss
- **Operation:** Gravity-in → gravity-out → constant water level

### Clean Water Lift (Only Pumps)

**Sump:**
- **Clean-water sump tank:** 300-500 gal
- **Level sensors:** Optional but recommended

**Pumps:**
- **2× identical low-head circulation pumps:** 1 duty, 1 backup
- **Sizing:** Slow turnover, not velocity
- **Installation:** Flexible unions + vibration isolation pads

---

## 💨 Gas Systems

### Air System (Redundant)

**Primary:**
- **Main blower:** Continuous duty
- **Air manifolds per zone:** Distribution control
- **Fine diffusers:** For larvae
- **Coarse diffusers:** For broodstock

**Backup:**
- **Backup air pump:** Battery or generator-backed
- **Automatic switchover:** Fail-safe operation

### Oxygen (Optional but Smart)

**Equipment:**
- **Low-head oxygen cone** OR inline diffuser
- **Small O₂ cylinder + regulator:** Emergency backup

**Purpose:**
- Boost dissolved oxygen during high-demand periods
- Emergency aeration during power outages
- Optimize growth rates

---

## 🌡️ Temperature Management

### Heating

**Equipment:**
- **2× inline electric water heaters:** Staged for efficiency
- **Mixing valve / bypass loop:** Temperature control
- **Thermostat:** ±1°C accuracy

### Thermal Mass (Inside Container)

**Thermal Bladder Walls:**
- **Segmented flat water bladders:** Framed and sealed
- **HDPE liner behind bladders:** Leak protection
- **Drain pan + leak sensor:** CRITICAL - early warning
- **Purpose:** Thermal mass, NOT structural walls

---

## 🎛️ Controls & Monitoring

### Sensors

- **Temperature:** 3 zones minimum (brood, spawn, larvae)
- **Dissolved Oxygen:** Brood + larvae zones
- **Water level:** Sump tank
- **Leak detectors:** Floor + bladder walls (CRITICAL)

### Controls

- **Simple PLC or aquarium controller:** Automation
- **Alarm outputs:** Text/email notifications
- **Manual overrides:** Emergency operations
- **Data logging:** Performance tracking

---

## 🚨 Emergency & Failure Modes

### Critical Systems (DO NOT SKIP)

1. **Gravity emergency overflow** from every tank
2. **Air-only survival mode** (no pumps required)
3. **Manual drain bypass** around filters
4. **Generator inlet** (even small portable)

### Failure Scenarios

| Failure Type | Primary Response | Backup System |
|--------------|------------------|---------------|
| Power loss | Air-only mode | Generator + battery backup |
| Pump failure | Gravity flow | Second pump (redundant) |
| Air loss | Passive aeration | Backup pump + manual air |
| Overflow | Emergency drains | Floor drains |
| Leak detection | Alarm + isolation | Floor drain pan + shut-off |
