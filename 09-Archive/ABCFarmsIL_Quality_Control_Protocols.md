## Introduction

### Purpose and Scope
This document establishes comprehensive quality control protocols for ABCFarmsIL's microgreens production. These protocols ensure that all products meet or exceed established quality standards, food safety requirements, and customer expectations. The protocols apply to all stages of production from seed selection through delivery and cover all varieties of microgreens produced by ABCFarmsIL.

### Quality Philosophy
ABCFarmsIL is committed to delivering the highest quality microgreens to our chef clients. Our quality philosophy is built on:
- Consistency in flavor, appearance, and shelf life
- Rigorous testing and inspection throughout the production process
- Data-driven decision making
- Continuous improvement
- Transparency with clients
- Zero tolerance for food safety risks

### Roles and Responsibilities

#### Quality Control Manager
- Oversees implementation of quality control protocols
- Conducts regular audits and inspections
- Reviews and analyzes quality data
- Leads continuous improvement initiatives
- Trains staff on quality procedures
- Reports quality metrics to leadership

#### Production Team
- Performs quality checks at designated control points
- Documents quality observations and measurements
- Identifies and reports quality issues
- Implements corrective actions as directed
- Participates in quality improvement initiatives

#### Delivery Team
- Conducts final quality checks before delivery
- Ensures proper handling during transport
- Collects client feedback on quality
- Reports any quality issues identified by clients

#### Leadership Team
- Establishes quality standards and expectations
- Provides resources for quality control activities
- Reviews quality performance metrics
- Approves major quality improvement initiatives
- Communicates quality commitment to stakeholders

## Quality Standards & Specifications

### Visual Quality Standards

#### Appearance Specifications
| Attribute | Standard | Measurement Method | Acceptable Variation |
|-----------|----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Color** | Vibrant, variety-specific coloration | Visual comparison to standard color chart | Slight variation in intensity acceptable |
| **Size/Height** | Variety-specific (see Appendix A) | Ruler measurement | ±0.5 inch from target height |
| **Density** | 90%+ coverage of growing medium | Visual assessment with coverage grid | Minimum 85% coverage |
| **Uniformity** | Even growth throughout tray | Visual assessment | No more than 10% variation in height |
| **Stem Thickness** | Variety-specific (see Appendix A) | Caliper measurement of sample | ±20% from target thickness |
| **Leaf Development** | Appropriate for variety and stage | Visual comparison to standard photos | Consistent with growth stage |

#### Defect Tolerances
| Defect Type | Tolerance Level | Inspection Method |
|-------------|----------------|------------------|
| **Yellowing** | <2% of total surface area | Visual inspection |
| **Browning/Decay** | 0% (zero tolerance) | Visual inspection |
| **Wilting** | <1% of plants | Visual inspection |
| **Stretching/Legginess** | <5% of plants | Visual inspection and measurement |
| **Physical Damage** | <2% of total surface area | Visual inspection |
| **Foreign Material** | 0% (zero tolerance) | Visual inspection |
| **Mold/Fungus** | 0% (zero tolerance) | Visual inspection |
| **Pest Damage** | 0% (zero tolerance) | Visual inspection |

### Shelf Life Standards

#### Minimum Shelf Life Requirements
| Product Type | Minimum Shelf Life | Storage Conditions | End-of-Life Criteria |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Cut Microgreens** | 7 days | 34-38°F, 90-95% humidity | Wilting, discoloration, or off-odor |

- Flavor testing at days 1, 3, 5, and 7

## Quality Control Checkpoints

### Seed Quality Control

#### Receiving Inspection
- **Frequency:** Every seed delivery
- **Responsible:** Production Manager
- **Procedure:**
  1. Verify seed variety and quantity against order
  2. Check packaging integrity
  3. Verify supplier documentation (organic certification, lot number, etc.)
  4. Inspect seeds for foreign material, damage, or contamination
  5. Document inspection results in Seed Receiving Log
  6. Assign internal lot number
  7. Retain sample from each lot

#### Seed Testing
- **Frequency:** Each new lot before use in production
- **Responsible:** Quality Control Manager
- **Procedure:**
  1. Conduct germination test (100 seeds)
  2. Calculate germination percentage after appropriate period
  3. Inspect seedlings for abnormalities
  4. Document results in Seed Testing Log
  5. Approve or reject lot based on results
  6. Adjust seeding rates if necessary based on germination percentage

### Growing Medium Quality Control

#### Receiving Inspection
- **Frequency:** Every medium delivery
- **Responsible:** Production Manager
- **Procedure:**
  1. Verify medium type and quantity against order
  2. Check packaging integrity
  3. Verify supplier documentation
  4. Inspect medium for foreign material, pests, or contamination
  5. Check moisture content
  6. Document inspection results in Medium Receiving Log

#### Medium Testing
- **Frequency:** Each new lot before use in production
- **Responsible:** Quality Control Manager
- **Procedure:**
  1. Test pH level (target: 5.8-6.2)
  2. Test water retention capacity
  3. Conduct small-scale growing test
  4. Document results in Medium Testing Log
  5. Approve or reject lot based on results

### Production Process Quality Control

#### Seeding Quality Check
- **Frequency:** Every tray at time of seeding
- **Responsible:** Production Team Member
- **Procedure:**
  1. Verify correct seed variety
  2. Check seeding density against standard
  3. Verify even distribution
  4. Document in Production Log
  5. Apply QR code or tray identifier

#### Germination Quality Check
- **Frequency:** Daily during germination phase
- **Responsible:** Production Team Member
- **Procedure:**
  1. Check moisture levels
  2. Assess germination progress
  3. Document germination rate
  4. Identify any abnormalities
  5. Update Production Log

#### Growing Phase Quality Checks
- **Frequency:** Daily during growing phase
- **Responsible:** Production Team Member
- **Procedure:**
  1. Assess color and vigor
  2. Measure sample plant height
  3. Check for any disease or pest issues
  4. Verify environmental conditions
  5. Document in Production Log
  6. Flag any issues for intervention

#### Pre-Harvest Quality Assessment
- **Frequency:** 24 hours before scheduled harvest
- **Responsible:** Quality Control Manager
- **Procedure:**
  1. Complete Pre-Harvest Quality Checklist (Appendix C)
  2. Assess readiness for harvest
  3. Conduct flavor test
  4. Measure height and density
  5. Approve or delay harvest
  6. Document decision and reasoning

### Harvest and Post-Harvest Quality Control

#### Harvest Quality Check
- **Frequency:** Every tray at harvest
- **Responsible:** Harvest Team Member
- **Procedure:**
  1. Verify tray meets harvest standards
  2. Check for any quality issues during cutting
  3. Remove any substandard portions
  4. Document yield and quality observations
  5. Flag any issues for quality review

#### Post-Harvest Inspection
- **Frequency:** Every batch after harvest
- **Responsible:** Quality Control Manager
- **Procedure:**
  1. Inspect random samples from batch
  2. Verify appearance standards are met
  3. Conduct flavor test
  4. Check for any physical damage
  5. Approve or reject batch
  6. Document inspection results

#### Packaging Quality Check
- **Frequency:** Every package
- **Responsible:** Packaging Team Member
- **Procedure:**
  1. Verify correct weight
  2. Check package integrity
  3. Verify label accuracy
  4. Inspect product appearance through packaging
  5. Document in Packaging Log

#### Final Quality Inspection
- **Frequency:** Every order before delivery
- **Responsible:** Quality Control Manager or Delivery Team Lead
- **Procedure:**
  1. Verify order accuracy
  2. Conduct final visual inspection
  3. Check temperature of storage/transport container
  4. Verify documentation is complete
  5. Approve for delivery
  6. Document final inspection

## Testing Procedures

### Germination Testing

#### Standard Germination Test
- **Purpose:** Verify seed viability and establish germination rate
- **Equipment:**
  - 100-cell seed tray or paper towel method
  - Standard growing medium
  - Spray bottle with filtered water
  - Labels and marker
  - Camera for documentation
- **Procedure:**
  1. Count out exactly 100 seeds from lot
  2. Prepare growing medium according to standard procedure
  3. Place one seed in each cell or evenly spaced on paper towel
  4. Moisten appropriately for seed type
  5. Cover if required by seed type
  6. Place in standard germination conditions
  7. Check daily and document progress
  8. Calculate final germination percentage after appropriate period (typically 3-7 days)
  9. Document results with photographs
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Minimum 90% germination rate for all varieties
  - Even, consistent germination timing
  - Normal seedling development

### Sensory Evaluation

#### Standard Flavor Testing
- **Purpose:** Evaluate flavor profile and consistency
- **Panel Requirements:**
  - Minimum 3 trained evaluators
  - Testing conducted before meals
  - No strong flavors consumed within 30 minutes of testing
- **Procedure:**
  1. Harvest samples according to standard procedure
  2. Prepare blind samples identified only by code
  3. Include control sample of known quality when possible
  4. Provide evaluation forms to panel members
  5. Evaluate on 5-point scale for key attributes:
     - Flavor intensity
     - Characteristic flavor
     - Texture
     - Freshness
     - Overall quality
  6. Calculate average scores
  7. Document comments and observations
  8. Compare to variety standards
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Minimum average score of 4.0 for overall quality
  - No individual attribute below 3.5
  - Consistency with established flavor profile

### Shelf Life Testing

#### Standard Shelf Life Test
- **Purpose:** Verify product meets minimum shelf life requirements
- **Equipment:**
  - Refrigerator set to 36°F (±2°F)
  - Humidity monitor
  - Standard packaging materials
  - Digital camera
  - Evaluation forms
- **Procedure:**
  1. Harvest and package product according to standard procedures
  2. Prepare minimum 7 identical packages
  3. Store in standard refrigeration conditions
  4. Evaluate one package each day for 7 days
  5. Document with photographs
  6. Rate appearance, texture, and aroma on 5-point scale
  7. Conduct flavor evaluation when appropriate
  8. Document results daily
  9. Determine effective shelf life (point at which quality drops below acceptable threshold)
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Minimum 7 days shelf life for cut microgreens
  - Minimum 10 days shelf life for living trays
  - Quality rating ≥3.0 at end of minimum shelf life period

### Microbial Testing

#### Standard Microbial Sampling
- **Purpose:** Verify product meets food safety standards
- **Frequency:** Monthly for each product type
- **Equipment:**
  - Sterile sampling tools
  - Sterile collection containers
  - Cooler with ice packs
  - Chain of custody forms
- **Procedure:**
  1. Collect samples using aseptic technique
  2. Take samples from multiple trays/packages
  3. Place in sterile container
  4. Label with date, variety, lot number
  5. Complete chain of custody documentation
  6. Transport to approved laboratory under temperature control
  7. Request standard microbial panel
  8. Document results upon receipt
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - Results within acceptable limits for all tests
  - No detection of pathogens
  - Consistent results over time

### Environmental Monitoring

#### Surface Sampling
- **Purpose:** Verify effectiveness of sanitation procedures
- **Frequency:** Weekly rotation of areas
- **Equipment:**
  - ATP monitoring system
  - Swabs
  - Sampling templates
  - Documentation forms
- **Procedure:**
  1. Identify sampling sites according to rotation schedule
  2. Collect samples after cleaning but before production
  3. Follow ATP meter manufacturer's instructions
  4. Record results immediately
  5. Mark any areas exceeding thresholds for re-cleaning
  6. Document all results in Environmental Monitoring Log
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - ATP readings below established thresholds for each area
  - Consistent or improving trends over time

#### Water Quality Testing
- **Purpose:** Verify water meets quality standards
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **Equipment:**
  - Sterile sample containers
  - pH meter
  - TDS meter
  - Test strips for chlorine (if applicable)
- **Procedure:**
  1. Collect water samples from production points of use
  2. Test pH, TDS, and chlorine levels on-site
  3. Send samples for microbial testing as scheduled
  4. Document all results in Water Quality Log
- **Acceptance Criteria:**
  - pH between 6.0-6.5
  - TDS within acceptable range for crop
  - No detectable pathogens
  - Chlorine levels within specification (if applicable)

## Non-Conformance Management

### Non-Conformance Classification

#### Critical Non-Conformance
- **Definition:** Issue that poses food safety risk, makes product unusable, or violates regulatory requirements
- **Examples:**
  - Pathogen detection
  - Chemical contamination
  - Foreign material contamination
  - Severe quality degradation
  - Mold or decay
- **Response Time:** Immediate action required
- **Authority:** Quality Control Manager must be notified immediately
- **Documentation:** Complete Non-Conformance Report within 24 hours

#### Major Non-Conformance
- **Definition:** Issue that significantly impacts product quality but does not pose safety risk
- **Examples:**
  - Significant appearance defects
  - Flavor profile deviation
  - Reduced shelf life
  - Packaging defects
  - Yield significantly below standard
- **Response Time:** Action required within 24 hours
- **Authority:** Production Manager can authorize disposition
- **Documentation:** Complete Non-Conformance Report within 48 hours

#### Minor Non-Conformance
- **Definition:** Issue that represents deviation from standards but has minimal impact on product quality
- **Examples:**
  - Slight color variation
  - Minor size inconsistency
  - Slight flavor variation
  - Cosmetic packaging issues
  - Documentation errors
- **Response Time:** Action required within production cycle
- **Authority:** Team Lead can authorize disposition
- **Documentation:** Record in Daily Quality Log

### Non-Conformance Response Protocol

#### Immediate Actions
1. **Identification & Isolation**
   - Clearly identify affected product
   - Segregate from conforming product
   - Label as "ON HOLD - QUALITY REVIEW"
   - Secure to prevent unintended use

2. **Notification**
   - Notify appropriate personnel based on severity
   - Provide initial assessment of issue
   - Document time and method of notification
   - Escalate if necessary

3. **Preliminary Assessment**
   - Determine scope of affected product
   - Assess potential impact
   - Determine if production should continue
   - Document initial findings

4. **Containment**
   - Identify any product already shipped
   - Place holds on inventory if necessary
   - Determine if recall is necessary
   - Implement immediate corrective actions

#### Root Cause Analysis
1. **Investigation Process**
   - Assemble investigation team
   - Collect relevant data and samples
   - Review production records
   - Interview involved personnel
   - Conduct necessary testing

2. **Analysis Methods**
   - 5-Why Analysis
   - Fishbone/Ishikawa Diagram
   - Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA)
   - Process mapping
   - Statistical analysis

3. **Documentation Requirements**
   - Clear statement of the problem
   - Evidence collected
   - Analysis methodology
   - Findings and conclusions
   - Verification of root cause

#### Corrective and Preventive Actions (CAPA)
1. **Corrective Action Development**
   - Address immediate issue
   - Eliminate root cause
   - Assign responsibility
   - Set implementation timeline
   - Define success criteria

2. **Preventive Action Development**
   - Identify similar processes at risk
   - Develop systemic improvements
   - Update procedures as needed
   - Implement training requirements
   - Establish monitoring mechanisms

3. **Implementation**
   - Execute actions according to plan
   - Document all activities
   - Collect evidence of completion
   - Verify implementation
   - Communicate changes to relevant personnel

4. **Effectiveness Verification**
   - Define verification method
   - Establish timeline for verification
   - Collect verification data
   - Analyze results
   - Document verification outcome

#### Product Disposition
1. **Evaluation Criteria**
   - Food safety impact
   - Quality impact
   - Regulatory compliance
   - Client specifications
   - Economic considerations

2. **Disposition Options**
   - Release: Product meets all requirements
   - Rework: Product can be brought into compliance
   - Downgrade: Product meets lower grade specifications
   - Donate: Product is safe but doesn't meet specifications
   - Discard: Product cannot be used

3. **Authorization Requirements**
   - Critical: Quality Control Manager and CEO approval
   - Major: Quality Control Manager approval
   - Minor: Production Manager approval

4. **Documentation**
   - Final disposition decision
   - Justification for decision
   - Quantity of product affected
   - Verification of disposition completion
   - Economic impact assessment

  - Develop improvement hypothesis
  - Create implementation plan

- **Do:**

  - Compare to expectations
  - Identify any unintended consequences
  - Determine effectiveness

- **Act:**
  - Standardize effective changes
  - Abandon ineffective changes
  - Identify next improvement cycle
  - Document lessons learned

- **Sort:**
  - Remove unnecessary items
  - Classify items by frequency of use
  - Eliminate obsolete tools/materials

- **Set in Order:**
  - Organize remaining items

  - Clean and inspect work areas
  - Identify sources of contamination
  - Establish cleaning standards

- **Standardize:**
  - Create consistent procedures
  - Develop visual controls
  - Establish regular audit process

- **Sustain:**
  - Train all team members
  - Incorporate into daily routine
  - Regular evaluation and reinforcement

## Audit Procedures

### Internal Audit Program

#### Audit Types and Frequency
- **Comprehensive Quality System Audit**
  - Frequency: Annual
  - Scope: All quality systems and procedures
  - Duration: 2-3 days
  - Team: Cross-functional audit team
  - Documentation: Formal audit report

- **Process-Specific Audits**
  - Frequency: Quarterly rotation (each process annually)
  - Scope: Individual processes (seeding, harvesting, etc.)
  - Duration: 4-8 hours per process
  - Team: Quality Control Manager and one team member
  - Documentation: Process audit report

- **Documentation Audits**
  - Frequency: Monthly
  - Scope: Records, logs, and documentation systems
  - Duration: 4-6 hours
  - Team: Quality Control Manager or designee
  - Documentation: Documentation audit report

- **Follow-up Audits**
  - Frequency: As needed based on findings
  - Scope: Specific areas with identified issues
  - Duration: Varies based on scope
  - Team: Original auditor when possible
  - Documentation: Follow-up audit report

#### Audit Planning
- **Pre-Audit Activities:**
  - Develop audit schedule (annual calendar)
  - Select and train audit team
  - Prepare audit checklist
  - Review previous audit findings
  - Notify affected departments
  - Gather relevant documentation

- **Audit Checklist Development:**
  - Based on current procedures and standards
  - Include regulatory requirements
  - Incorporate previous findings
  - Add specific focus areas
  - Include verification methods
  - Provide scoring criteria

- **Audit Team Requirements:**
  - Independence from area being audited
  - Trained in audit procedures
  - Knowledge of quality standards
  - Objectivity and attention to detail
  - Documentation skills

#### Audit Execution
- **Opening Meeting:**
  - Introduce audit team
  - Confirm audit scope and objectives
  - Review audit process
  - Establish communication protocols
  - Address questions or concerns

- **Data Collection Methods:**
  - Document review
  - Direct observation
  - Employee interviews
  - Process testing
  - Records sampling
  - Physical inspection

- **Evidence Requirements:**
  - Objective evidence for all findings
  - Multiple sources when possible
  - Documentation of observations
  - Photographic evidence when appropriate
  - Sampling methodology documentation

- **Closing Meeting:**
  - Summarize preliminary findings
  - Clarify any misunderstandings
  - Discuss timeline for final report
  - Outline next steps
  - Acknowledge participation

#### Audit Reporting and Follow-up
- **Audit Report Content:**
  - Executive summary
  - Scope and objectives
  - Methodology
  - Conformance summary
  - Detailed findings by category
  - Recommendations
  - Required actions
  - Timeline for response

- **Finding Classification:**
  - Critical: Significant food safety risk or regulatory violation
  - Major: System failure or significant quality risk
  - Minor: Isolated incident or documentation issue
  - Observation: Improvement opportunity

- **Corrective Action Process:**
  - Develop action plan within 10 days
  - Address root causes
  - Establish implementation timeline
  - Assign responsibilities
  - Define verification method
  - Document in CAPA system

- **Verification Process:**
  - Follow-up audit for critical/major findings
  - Documentation review for minor findings
  - Verification timing based on severity
  - Documentation of verification results
  - Closure of findings when verified

### Supplier Audits

#### Supplier Qualification Audit
- **Timing:** Before approving new supplier
- **Scope:**
  - Facility and operations assessment
  - Quality management systems
  - Food safety programs
  - Regulatory compliance
  - Sustainability practices
- **Methodology:**
  - On-site audit preferred
  - Documentation review
  - Process observation
  - Testing verification
  - Management interviews
- **Outcome:**
  - Approved: Meets all requirements
  - Conditionally Approved: Minor issues with correction plan
  - Not Approved: Major issues identified

#### Routine Supplier Monitoring
- **Frequency:** Annual for critical suppliers
- **Methods:**
  - On-site audit (high-risk suppliers)
  - Self-assessment questionnaire
  - Certificate review
  - Product testing
  - Performance metrics review
- **Evaluation Criteria:**
  - Product quality consistency
  - On-time delivery performance
  - Issue response effectiveness
  - Continuous improvement evidence
  - Compliance maintenance

#### Supplier Corrective Action Process
- **Initiation Criteria:**
  - Audit findings
  - Product quality issues
  - Delivery performance issues
  - Documentation non-compliance
- **Process:**
  1. Issue formal SCAR (Supplier Corrective Action Request)
  2. Require root cause analysis
  3. Review proposed corrective actions
  4. Establish verification method
  5. Follow up according to timeline
  6. Document in supplier management system
