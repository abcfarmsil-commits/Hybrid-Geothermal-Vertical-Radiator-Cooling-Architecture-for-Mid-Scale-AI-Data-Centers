## Introduction

### Purpose and Scope
This document outlines the comprehensive inventory management system for ABCFarmsIL. The system is designed to ensure optimal inventory levels, minimize waste, control costs, and support efficient operations. It covers all materials, supplies, and finished products used in the microgreens production process from seed to delivery.

### Inventory Management Philosophy
ABCFarmsIL's inventory management philosophy is built on the following principles:
- Just-in-time inventory to minimize storage requirements
- First-in, first-out (FIFO) usage to ensure freshness
- Data-driven forecasting to optimize ordering
- Waste minimization through careful planning
- Full traceability from seed to delivery
- Continuous improvement through inventory analytics

### Business Impact
An effective inventory management system directly impacts:
- Cash flow through optimized inventory investment
- Product quality through proper rotation and storage
- Customer satisfaction through consistent availability
- Operational efficiency through streamlined processes
- Profitability through waste reduction and cost control
- Sustainability through minimized resource consumption

### Identification & Tracking Methods

#### Barcode System
- **Implementation:**
  - Unique barcode for each inventory item
  - Barcode labels for storage locations
  - Barcode scanner for transactions
  - Integration with inventory database
- **Usage Protocol:**
  - Scan location, then item, then quantity for all transactions
  - Verification prompt for unusual quantities
  - Error notification for incorrect scans
  - Backup manual entry option

#### QR Code System for Production Trays
- **Implementation:**
  - Unique QR code for each production tray
  - Contains variety, seeding date, expected harvest date
  - Scannable with smartphone or tablet
  - Links to production record in database
- **Usage Protocol:**
  - Generate and apply QR code at seeding
  - Scan at each production milestone
  - Scan at harvest for yield tracking
  - Scan returned trays for cycle tracking

#### Lot Tracking System
- **Implementation:**
  - Assign lot numbers to all incoming materials
  - Track lot numbers through production process
  - Link finished product to input lot numbers
  - Enable trace-back and trace-forward capabilities
- **Usage Protocol:**
  - Record lot numbers at receiving
  - Track lot usage in production
  - Maintain lot history for minimum 2 years
  - Test trace-back quarterly

#### RFID System (Future Implementation)
- **Potential Applications:**
  - Reusable container tracking
  - Equipment location tracking
  - Automated inventory counts
  - Temperature monitoring during transport
- **Implementation Considerations:**
  - Cost-benefit analysis
  - Integration with existing systems
  - Tag durability in growing environment
  - Reader placement and coverage

### Inventory Locations & Organization

#### Location Coding System
- **Structure:**
  - Area code (2 letters)
  - Aisle number (2 digits)
  - Shelf/rack number (2 digits)
  - Position number (2 digits)
  - Example: GR-01-02-03 (Growing Room, Aisle 1, Rack 2, Position 3)
- **Implementation:**
  - Visible location labels with barcodes
  - Map of location codes
  - Location directory in inventory system
  - Regular validation of location accuracy

#### Storage Organization Principles
- **General Principles:**
  - Store similar items together
  - Place high-use items in accessible locations
  - Consider item size, weight, and handling requirements
  - Maintain separation between incompatible items
  - Organize to facilitate FIFO rotation

- **Specific Organization Methods:**
  - Seeds: Alphabetical by variety in climate-controlled cabinet
  - Growing media: By type on pallets with oldest in front
  - Packaging: By type and size on shelving
  - Cleaning supplies: By function with safety considerations
  - Finished products: By harvest date and variety in cooler

#### Inventory Movement Rules
- **Receipt to Storage:**
  - Inspect and verify quantity and quality
  - Enter into inventory system
  - Apply lot tracking if applicable
  - Move to designated storage location
  - Rotate stock to maintain FIFO

- **Storage to Production:**
  - Generate pick list from production plan
  - Retrieve oldest suitable inventory first
  - Scan or record removal from inventory
  - Transport to production area
  - Return unused items to storage

- **Production to Finished Goods:**
  - Record yield and create finished goods inventory
  - Apply appropriate labeling and tracking
  - Move to designated storage location
  - Update inventory system

- **Finished Goods to Delivery:**
  - Generate pick list from orders
  - Retrieve according to FIFO and order requirements
  - Scan or record removal from inventory
  - Load for delivery
  - Update inventory system

## Ordering & Procurement Processes

### Inventory Planning

#### Demand Forecasting
- **Forecasting Methods:**
  - Historical usage analysis
  - Seasonal trend adjustment
  - Growth projection factors
  - Client-specific forecasting
  - New product introduction planning
- **Forecast Periods:**
  - Weekly detailed forecast
  - Monthly aggregate forecast
  - Quarterly trend analysis
  - Annual planning forecast
- **Forecast Review Process:**
  - Weekly review of short-term forecast
  - Monthly review of medium-term forecast
  - Quarterly review of long-term forecast
  - Documentation of forecast accuracy

#### Par Level Determination
- **Par Level Factors:**
  - Average daily/weekly usage
  - Lead time for replenishment
  - Safety stock requirements
  - Storage capacity constraints
  - Shelf life limitations
  - Supplier minimum order quantities
  - Volume discount thresholds
  - Seasonal variations
- **Par Level Calculation:**
  - Base par = (Average daily usage × Lead time days) + Safety stock
  - Adjusted par = Base par adjusted for constraints and opportunities
  - Review and update par levels quarterly
  - Document par level rationale

#### Reorder Point Calculation
- **Reorder Point Factors:**
  - Average daily/weekly usage
  - Lead time for replenishment
  - Safety stock requirements
  - Order processing time
  - Delivery schedule constraints
- **Reorder Point Calculation:**
  - Reorder point = (Average daily usage × (Lead time days + Processing days)) + Safety stock
  - Set system alerts for approaching reorder points
  - Review and update reorder points quarterly
  - Document exceptions and special cases

### Reconciliation Process

#### Variance Analysis
- **Variance Calculation:**
  - Quantity variance = Physical count - System quantity
  - Value variance = Quantity variance × Average unit cost
  - Percentage variance = (Quantity variance ÷ System quantity) × 100

- **Variance Categories:**
  - Acceptable variance: ±2% for most items
  - Minor variance: ±2-5%
  - Major variance: ±5-10%
  - Critical variance: >10% or high-value items

- **Investigation Thresholds:**
  - All critical variances
  - Major variances over $100 in value
  - Pattern of minor variances
  - Any variance in controlled items

#### Adjustment Procedures
- **Documentation Requirements:**
  - Inventory Adjustment Form
  - Variance calculation
  - Investigation findings
  - Root cause determination
  - Corrective action plan
  - Approval signatures

- **Approval Levels:**
  - Minor adjustments (<$100): Inventory Manager
  - Medium adjustments ($100-$500): Operations Manager
  - Major adjustments (>$500): CEO
  - Pattern of adjustments: Operations Manager regardless of value

- **System Updates:**
  - Enter approved adjustments in system
  - Document adjustment reason code
  - Link to adjustment documentation
  - Update average costs if necessary
  - Generate adjustment report

#### Root Cause Analysis
- **Common Causes:**
  - Receiving errors
  - Usage recording errors
  - Shrinkage/theft
  - Damage/spoilage
  - Miscounting
  - System errors

- **Analysis Process:**
  - Review transaction history
  - Interview relevant personnel

  - Identify process weaknesses

- **Corrective Action Development:**
  - Address specific incident

  - Follow-up verification

### Inventory Optimization

#### Safety Stock Calculation
- **Calculation Factors:**
  - Demand variability
  - Supply variability
  - Service level targets
  - Lead time
  - Criticality of item
  - Storage constraints

- **Calculation Method:**
  - Safety Stock = Z × σ × √LT
    - Z = Service level factor
    - σ = Standard deviation of demand
    - LT = Lead time

- **Review and Adjustment:**
  - Monthly review of safety stock levels
  - Adjustment based on performance
  - Seasonal modifications
  - Service level achievement analysis
  - Storage capacity considerations

#### Economic Order Quantity
- **EOQ Calculation:**
  - EOQ = √(2DS/H)
    - D = Annual demand
    - S = Order cost
    - H = Holding cost per unit

- **Application Considerations:**
  - Balance between ordering and holding costs
  - Minimum order quantities
  - Quantity discount thresholds
  - Shelf life constraints
  - Storage capacity limitations

- **Implementation:**
  - Calculate EOQ for high-value items
  - Round to practical order quantities
  - Review and adjust quarterly
  - Document exceptions to EOQ
  - Measure impact on inventory value

#### Inventory Turnover Goals
- **Turnover Targets by Category:**
  - Seeds: 4-6 turns per year
  - Growing media: 12 turns per year
  - Packaging: 12 turns per year
  - Cleaning supplies: 6 turns per year
  - Finished products: 52 turns per year (weekly)

- **Calculation Method:**
  - Inventory Turnover = Cost of Goods Sold ÷ Average Inventory Value
  - Days of Supply = 365 ÷ Inventory Turnover

- **Performance Tracking:**
  - Monthly turnover calculation
  - Comparison to targets
  - Trend analysis
  - Slow-moving inventory identification

### Key Performance Indicators

#### Inventory Efficiency KPIs

  - Target: Category-specific (see Inventory Turnover Goals)

- **Days of Supply:**
  - Calculation: 365 ÷ Inventory Turnover
  - Target: Category-specific
  - Reporting Frequency: Monthly
  - Visualization: Bar chart by category

- **Carrying Cost Percentage:**
  - Calculation: Annual Inventory Carrying Cost ÷ Average Inventory Value
  - Target: <20% of inventory value
  - Reporting Frequency: Quarterly
  - Visualization: Pie chart of cost components

- **Inventory Accuracy:**
  - Calculation: (1 - |Count Variance| ÷ Total Count) × 100
  - Target: >98%
  - Reporting Frequency: Monthly
  - Visualization: Accuracy percentage with trend

- **Fill Rate:**
  - Calculation: Orders Filled Completely ÷ Total Orders
  - Target: >98%
  - Reporting Frequency: Weekly
  - Visualization: Weekly trend with target line

- **Line Fill Rate:**
  - Calculation: Order Lines Filled Completely ÷ Total Order Lines
  - Target: >99%
  - Reporting Frequency: Weekly
  - Visualization: Weekly trend with target line

- **Stockout Frequency:**
  - Calculation: Stockout Incidents ÷ Time Period
  - Target: <1% of SKUs per month
  - Reporting Frequency: Monthly
  - Visualization: Pareto chart of stockout items

- **On-Time Delivery:**
  - Calculation: On-Time Deliveries ÷ Total Deliveries
  - Target: >98%
  - Reporting Frequency: Weekly
  - Visualization: Weekly trend with target line

#### Cost Management KPIs

  - Visualization: Trend line with sales overlay

- **Obsolescence Rate:**
  - Calculation: Value of Obsolete Inventory ÷ Total Inventory Value

- **Shrinkage Rate:**
