# **ABCFARMSIL: Equity, Sustainability & Local Resilience Frames (Phase 0.8 → Phase 2\)**

These frames **run through all phases**. They are not add-ons.

---

## **1️⃣ EquITY FRAME — Access, Ownership, Skill Transfer**

**Core principle:**

*People closest to the system should be able to understand it, operate it, and eventually own part of it.*

### **How Equity Shows Up by Phase**

### **Phase 0.8**

* Founder-operated, transparent economics  
* Open documentation of workflows  
* Entry-level operational tasks clearly defined  
* No credential barriers to participation

**Equity signal:**  
*This system can be learned without elite capital or degrees.*

---

### **Phase 1**

* SOPs written for non-experts  
* Farm tech role becomes trainable  
* Skills become portable (irrigation, sanitation, harvest logistics)

**Equity signal:**  
*Workforce-relevant skills with real labor market value.*

---

### **Phase 2**

* Specialized roles emerge (fungi lead, systems tech)  
* Clear paths for:  
  * Long-term employment  
  * Revenue sharing  
  * Equity participation (when appropriate)

**Equity signal:**  
*Knowledge → responsibility → ownership.*

This is where dropping the word **equity** was correct. You’re signaling maturity, not desperation.

---

## **2️⃣ SUSTAINABILITY FRAME — Measured, Not Marketed**

**Core principle:**

*If it can’t be measured, it doesn’t count.*

### **What You Explicitly Avoid**

* “Green” claims without numbers  
* Carbon-offset storytelling  
* Overbuilt tech dependency

### **What You Emphasize Instead**

### **Phase 0.8**

* Reduced transport miles (local sales)  
* Low-energy germination systems  
* Minimal refrigeration (42–44°F, not freezing)

**Sustainability signal:**  
*Efficiency beats novelty.*

---

### **Phase 1**

* Water reuse mapping  
* Power discipline (what actually needs electricity)  
* Waste stream tracking (what leaves the site)

**Sustainability signal:**  
*Operational restraint.*

---

### **Phase 2**

* Dedicated vermicomposting module  
* Substrate cycling between fungi and soil  
* Closed-loop nutrient accounting

**Sustainability signal:**  
*Biology replaces disposal.*

This is where Kevin leaned in — because it’s real systems ecology, not branding.

---

## **3️⃣ LOCAL RESILIENCE FRAME — Anti-Fragile, Not Scalable-for-VC**

This is subtle but powerful.

**Core principle:**

*Systems should fail gracefully, not catastrophically.*

### **How Resilience Is Designed In**

### **Modularity**

* Each container is independently operable  
* Failure in fungi ≠ failure in microgreens  
* Dome is added *after* core stability

### **Redundancy**

* Manual overrides  
* Simple environmental controls  
* No single point of biological failure

### **Local Fit**

* Works on brownfields  
* Works with part-time labor  
* Works without continuous internet or cloud dependency

**Resilience signal:**  
*This system survives stress.*
