## 30-Second Pitch (For Calls/In-Person)

> "I've designed a cooling system for data centers that uses building height and gravity instead of powered fans. It's a vertical radiator tower that reduces cooling energy by 30-40% with fewer moving parts and lower water use. I'm looking for a pilot host to validate the numbers, or a manufacturer to evaluate the design."

## Key Talking Points

**If they ask "How does it work?":**
> "Hot water rises through a 60-foot vertical tower. Radiator fins reject heat to ambient air. Geothermal loop stabilizes temperature. Gravity returns cooled water to the data hall. No pumps for the main circulation - just thermal head."

**If they ask "What's the catch?":**
> "It requires building height - you need a tower or multi-story building. And the numbers are projected, not validated. That's why I'm looking for a pilot host."

**If they ask "Why hasn't this been done?":**
> "Cooling towers are cheap and proven. Data centers haven't had enough pressure on efficiency until now. With AI workloads doubling power density and hyperscalers facing net-zero mandates, the math is changing."

**If they ask "What do you need?":**
> "A facility with high cooling costs and interest in validating something new. I'd install at reduced cost in exchange for data and a case study."
