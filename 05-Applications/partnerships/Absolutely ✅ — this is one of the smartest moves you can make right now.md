# **🎓 ABCFARMSIL 365 Curriculum Block — “Circular Food Systems Lab”**

**Program Context:**  
Certificate or elective module within Environmental Science, Sustainable Agriculture, Urban Studies, or Workforce Development.  
Duration: 12–15 weeks (adaptable to semester or short intensive).

---

## **MODULE 1 — GERMINATION ENGINES (Microgreens Systems)**

**Theme:** Controlled Biology \+ Clean Infrastructure

### **Learning Goals**

* Understand controlled-environment food production.  
* Analyze how clean/dirty separation supports food safety.  
* Measure energy and water use in a closed system.  
* Practice seeding, misting, lighting, and harvest scheduling.

### **Core Skills**

* Tray system setup and calibration  
* Light and moisture cycle management  
* Data logging (temperature, humidity, growth rate)  
* Food-safe handling and sanitation (Return Cycle SOP)

### **Lab Prompts**

1. Record germination rates for two species under different light spectra.  
2. Measure and compare water usage per tray cycle (liters/tray).  
3. Calculate break-even points for a 40-tray weekly rotation.

### **Learning Outcomes**

Students can:

* Operate a small-scale biological reactor with measurable consistency.  
* Demonstrate data collection for MRV (Measurement, Reporting, Verification).  
* Explain how micro-scale production connects to regional food equity goals.

---

## **MODULE 2 — FUNGI SYSTEMS (Mycology & Closed-Loop Substrate Use)**

**Theme:** Applied Mycology \+ Low-Energy Protein Production

### **Learning Goals**

* Understand fungal life cycles and substrate inoculation.  
* Model containerized fruiting environments.  
* Examine microbial succession between feedstock and soil recovery.

### **Core Skills**

* Substrate preparation and sanitation (steam or hot soak)  
* Bucket inoculation and labeling (QR system integration)  
* Fruiting environment monitoring (CO₂, humidity, temperature)  
* Simple harvest and data logging

### **Lab Prompts**

1. Compare colonization rates between two oyster species under identical conditions.  
2. Record environmental data and correlate with fruit yield.  
3. Trace material flow from microgreens waste → fungi substrate → vermi feedstock.

### **Learning Outcomes**

Students can:

* Manage a stable fruiting cycle in a closed environment.  
* Track biological transformation from waste to protein.  
* Articulate fungi’s role in regenerative nutrient cycling.

---

## **MODULE 3 — VERMI MODULE (Soil Biology & Nutrient Recovery)**

**Theme:** Waste-to-Value and Systems Ecology

### **Learning Goals**

* Understand biological digestion and compost ecosystems.  
* Evaluate design trade-offs in low-mechanics vermicomposting.  
* Quantify nutrient flow and soil-building efficiency.

### **Core Skills**

* Feedstock preparation and staged bed management  
* Moisture and temperature regulation  
* Harvesting and screening vermicast  
* Recording biological stability indicators

### **Lab Prompts**

1. Measure mass reduction over 14 days of vermicomposting.  
2. Analyze pH, temperature, and moisture trends across stages 1–3.  
3. Develop a system diagram showing circular resource flow among all three modules.

### **Learning Outcomes**

Students can:

* Operate and monitor a modular vermicomposting unit.  
* Explain interdependencies between clean and dirty systems.  
* Present a small-scale circular economy model with empirical data.

---

## **🧩 Capstone Integration Project**

**Goal:** Demonstrate the complete adaptive biocycle loop.

**Prompt:**  
Design and present a 365-day operation plan that links all three modules — including water, power, and nutrient flows — and identify one measurable sustainability metric (energy, waste reduction, or water reuse).

**Deliverables:**

* Visual system map (digital or whiteboard)  
* Short presentation / poster / one-page MRV summary  
* Reflection essay: “Resilience, Not Fragility — What I Learned About Systems That Survive”
