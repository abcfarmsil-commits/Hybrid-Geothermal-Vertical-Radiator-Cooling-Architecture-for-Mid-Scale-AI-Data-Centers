# Vertical Radiator Tower Cooling System
## Hybrid Geothermal + Gravity-Assisted Heat Rejection

---

## The Problem

Data centers spend **30-40% of total energy on cooling**. Traditional systems rely on:
- Power-hungry chillers and fans
- Water-intensive cooling towers
- Compressors that fail on peak summer days

**Cooling is the largest operating cost after IT load itself.**

---

## The Solution

A **hybrid vertical radiator + geothermal cooling architecture** that uses:
- **Building height as thermal head** (60 ft vertical drop)
- **Gravity-assisted flow** instead of pumped pressure
- **Geothermal stabilization** for peak-load smoothing
- **Dry-first heat rejection** with optional water assist

**Result: Reduced parasitic power, lower water use, fewer moving parts.**

---

## How It Works

```
Hot return (50°C) → Vertical Tower → Radiator Array → Geothermal Loop → Cool supply (40°C)
                              ↓
                    Passive convection cooling
                    (stack effect + thermal head)
```

1. **Hot water rises naturally** through vertical tower
2. **Radiator array rejects heat** to ambient air (dry mode)
3. **Geothermal loop** provides temperature stabilization
4. **Gravity returns cooled water** to data hall
