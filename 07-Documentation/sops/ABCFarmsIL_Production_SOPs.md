## Introduction

### Purpose of This Document
This Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) manual provides comprehensive guidelines for all aspects of microgreens production at ABCFarmsIL. These procedures are designed to ensure consistent product quality, maximize yield, maintain food safety standards, and optimize operational efficiency.

### Core Production Philosophy
ABCFarmsIL is committed to producing the highest quality microgreens using sustainable practices. Our production philosophy centers on:
- Ultra-fresh, just-in-time harvesting
- Sustainable growing practices
- Consistent quality and flavor profiles
- Zero-waste operations
- Continuous improvement and innovation

### How to Use This Manual
- All production team members must thoroughly review this manual before beginning work
- SOPs should be followed precisely to ensure consistency
- Any deviations must be approved by the Production Manager
- Suggestions for improvements should be submitted through the Continuous Improvement Form
- This manual will be updated quarterly or as needed to reflect process improvements

## Facility Preparation & Maintenance

### Daily Facility Checklist

#### Morning Procedures (6:00-7:00 AM)
1. **Environmental Controls**
   - Check and record temperature (target: 65-75°F)
   - Check and record humidity (target: 50-60%)
   - Verify proper air circulation
   - Ensure adequate lighting function

2. **Water Systems**
   - Check water filtration system
   - Verify proper pH levels (target: 6.0-6.5)
   - Ensure adequate water pressure
   - Check for any leaks or issues

3. **Growing Area**
   - Visually inspect all growing racks
   - Check for any signs of contamination
   - Ensure proper spacing between trays
   - Verify proper light positioning

#### Evening Procedures (5:00-6:00 PM)
1. **Environmental Controls**
   - Adjust temperature for overnight (target: 62-68°F)
   - Check humidity levels (target: 50-60%)
   - Verify timer settings for lights
   - Record end-of-day environmental readings

2. **Growing Area**
   - Perform final visual inspection of all trays
   - Identify any trays needing attention the next day
   - Ensure all equipment is properly stored
   - Verify that all areas are clean and organized

### Weekly Facility Maintenance

#### Equipment Maintenance (Monday)
1. **Lighting Systems**
   - Check all grow lights for proper function
   - Clean light fixtures and remove dust
   - Replace any bulbs showing signs of dimming
   - Verify timer settings and light cycles

2. **Irrigation Systems**
   - Clean all spray nozzles and irrigation lines
   - Check for proper water distribution
   - Verify pump function and pressure
   - Clean water filters and reservoirs

#### Deep Cleaning (Sunday)
1. **Growing Areas**
   - Remove all trays from one section at a time
   - Clean and sanitize rack surfaces
   - Clean floors and walls in growing area
   - Check for any signs of mold or pests

2. **Work Areas**
   - Deep clean all work surfaces
   - Sanitize all tools and equipment
   - Organize storage areas
   - Restock supplies as needed

### Monthly Facility Maintenance

1. **HVAC System**
   - Check and replace air filters
   - Clean air intake and exhaust vents
   - Verify thermostat function
   - Inspect for any unusual noises or operation

2. **Plumbing System**
   - Descale water lines if needed
   - Check for any slow drains or leaks
   - Clean all floor drains
   - Verify backflow prevention devices

3. **Electrical System**
   - Check all electrical connections
   - Test emergency lighting
   - Verify generator function (if applicable)
   - Inspect for any damaged cords or plugs

4. **Structure**
   - Inspect walls, ceiling, and floors for damage
   - Check door seals and weather stripping
   - Inspect for any signs of water intrusion
   - Verify security systems function

## Seed Selection & Storage

### Seed Sourcing Standards

1. **Approved Suppliers**
   - Purchase only from approved suppliers listed in Appendix A
   - Require certificate of analysis for each seed lot
   - Verify organic certification when applicable
   - Request germination test results for each batch

2. **Seed Quality Requirements**
   - Minimum 90% germination rate
   - Free from chemical treatments
   - Verified pathogen testing
   - Proper variety authentication

3. **Receiving Procedures**
   - Inspect packaging for damage upon arrival
   - Verify seed count/weight against order
   - Check for moisture or contamination
   - Document lot numbers and supplier information

### Seed Storage Protocol

1. **Storage Conditions**
   - Temperature: 50-65°F (10-18°C)
   - Humidity: Below 40%
   - Dark environment
   - Good air circulation

2. **Container Requirements**
   - Air-tight, food-grade containers
   - Clearly labeled with:
     - Seed variety
     - Supplier
     - Lot number
     - Receiving date
     - Germination rate
     - Expiration date

3. **Inventory Management**
   - First-in, first-out (FIFO) usage
   - Weekly inventory count
   - Minimum 2-week supply maintained
   - Maximum 6-month storage for most varieties

4. **Quality Monitoring**
   - Monthly germination testing of stored seeds
   - Visual inspection for moisture or pests
   - Discard any seeds showing quality decline
   - Document all quality checks

### Seed Preparation for Production

1. **Measurement Procedures**
   - Use calibrated digital scale
   - Follow seed density chart (Appendix B)
   - Document seed weight for each tray
   - Adjust seeding rate based on germination tests

2. **Pre-Soak Protocol (When Required)**
   - Use filtered water at room temperature
   - Follow variety-specific soak times (Appendix C)
   - Use food-grade containers for soaking
   - Rinse seeds thoroughly after soaking

3. **Sanitization (When Required)**
   - Use approved food-grade hydrogen peroxide solution (3%)
   - Soak seeds for 5 minutes
   - Rinse thoroughly with filtered water
   - Document sanitization process

## Growing Medium Preparation

### Medium Selection

1. **Approved Growing Media**
   - Organic coco coir
   - Hemp growing mats
   - Soil-less organic mix (for specific varieties)
   - Biodegradable fiber pads

2. **Medium Specifications**
   - Free from contaminants and foreign materials
   - Proper water retention capacity
   - Appropriate pH (5.8-6.2)
   - Certified organic when possible

3. **Storage Requirements**
   - Store in cool, dry location
   - Keep sealed until use
   - Store off the floor on pallets
   - Maintain separation from chemicals and cleaning supplies

### Medium Preparation Process

1. **Coco Coir Preparation**
   - Measure dry coir by weight according to tray size
   - Hydrate with filtered water in clean container
   - Allow to expand for 15-30 minutes
   - Break apart any clumps for even consistency
   - Drain excess water before use

2. **Hemp Mat Preparation**
   - Pre-moisten mats with pH-balanced water
   - Allow to absorb water for 5 minutes
   - Gently press to remove excess water
   - Cut to fit tray size if necessary

3. **Soil-less Mix Preparation**
   - Mix components according to recipe (Appendix D)
   - Moisten to 70% water capacity
   - Allow to sit for 10 minutes
   - Fluff and break apart any clumps

### Tray Preparation

1. **Tray Selection**
   - Use appropriate tray size for variety
   - Inspect trays for cracks or damage
   - Verify trays are properly cleaned and sanitized
   - Use only food-grade materials

2. **Tray Setup**
   - For standard trays:
     - Place 1-2 inches of prepared medium in tray
     - Level surface evenly
     - Create slight depression in center for even water distribution
   - For 6-square trays:
     - Install dividers securely
     - Place appropriate medium in each section
     - Label each section according to variety plan

3. **Pre-Seeding Moisture**
   - Mist prepared trays with filtered water
   - Ensure even moisture throughout medium
   - Avoid oversaturation
   - Allow surface to become slightly tacky before seeding

## Seeding Process

### Preparation for Seeding

1. **Workspace Setup**
   - Clean and sanitize all work surfaces
   - Gather all necessary tools and materials:
     - Prepared trays
     - Measured seeds
     - Spray bottles
     - Seed shakers or spreaders
     - Labels and markers
     - Production log
   - Wash hands and put on clean gloves
   - Ensure adequate lighting for precision work

2. **Seed Density Guidelines**
   - Follow variety-specific seeding rates (Appendix E)
   - Standard densities:
     - Small seeds (amaranth, broccoli): 1.0-1.5 oz/10"×20" tray
     - Medium seeds (radish, mustard): 2.0-3.0 oz/10"×20" tray
     - Large seeds (sunflower, pea): 6.0-8.0 oz/10"×20" tray
   - Adjust based on germination rate testing
   - Document actual seed weight used

### Seeding Techniques

1. **Even Distribution Method**
   - For small/medium seeds:
     - Use seed shaker or salt shaker
     - Apply in overlapping passes
     - Aim for even single-layer coverage
     - Seeds should be close but not piled
   - For large seeds:
     - Place individually by hand
     - Maintain consistent spacing
     - Create a single layer with seeds touching

2. **6-Square Tray Technique**
   - Seed one section at a time
   - Use divider guards to prevent cross-contamination
   - Label each section immediately after seeding
   - Verify correct variety placement against production plan

3. **Post-Seeding Treatment**
   - Mist lightly with filtered water
   - For light-sensitive seeds, cover with another tray
   - For seeds requiring weight, place weighted tray on top
   - Apply variety-specific treatments (Appendix F)

### Labeling and Documentation

1. **Tray Labeling Requirements**
   - Each tray must be labeled with:
     - Variety name
     - Seeding date
     - Expected harvest date
     - Batch/lot number
     - Initials of seeder

2. **QR Code System (When Implemented)**
   - Generate QR code for each tray
   - Attach to tray edge
   - Scan to enter into tracking system
   - Link to complete production record

3. **Production Log Entry**
   - Record in daily production log:
     - Date and time
     - Varieties and quantities seeded
     - Seed lot numbers used
     - Growing medium used
     - Environmental conditions
     - Notes or observations

## Germination Phase

### Germination Environment

1. **Optimal Conditions by Variety**
   - Temperature: 65-75°F (18-24°C)
   - Humidity: 80-90%
   - Light: Variety-specific (see Appendix G)
   - Air circulation: Minimal during germination

2. **Blackout Period**
   - Duration: Variety-specific (see Appendix G)
   - Standard ranges:
     - Small seeds: 2-3 days
     - Medium seeds: 2-4 days
     - Large seeds: 1-3 days
   - Stack trays or cover as specified
   - Maintain weight on trays requiring pressure

3. **Environmental Monitoring**
   - Check temperature twice daily
   - Verify humidity levels twice daily
   - Document all readings in germination log
   - Adjust conditions as needed

### Germination Care

1. **Moisture Management**
   - Check moisture levels twice daily
   - Mist as needed to maintain consistent moisture
   - Avoid overwatering which can lead to mold
   - Use bottom watering for sensitive varieties

2. **Daily Inspection**
   - Check for signs of germination
   - Monitor for mold or fungal growth
   - Verify even moisture distribution
   - Document germination progress

3. **Transition from Blackout**
   - Remove covering at first sign of germination for most varieties
   - For varieties requiring extended blackout, follow specific protocol
   - Gradually introduce light to prevent shock
   - Move to growing area when 90% germination is achieved

### Germination Troubleshooting

1. **Poor Germination Response**
   - If less than 70% germination after expected period:
     - Check moisture levels
     - Verify temperature conditions
     - Inspect for seed quality issues
     - Document issue and response

2. **Mold Prevention**
   - At first sign of mold:
     - Improve air circulation
     - Reduce humidity
     - Consider hydrogen peroxide spray (1%)
     - Document treatment and results

3. **Germination Documentation**
   - Record germination rate for each tray
   - Note any issues or variations
   - Document any corrective actions taken
   - Update expected harvest date if needed

## Growing Phase

### Growing Environment

1. **Optimal Growing Conditions**
   - Temperature: 65-70°F (18-21°C)
   - Humidity: 40-60%
   - Light: 14-16 hours daily
   - Air circulation: Moderate, constant

2. **Lighting Specifications**
   - LED grow lights with full spectrum
   - Height: 12-18 inches above plant canopy
   - Duration: 14-16 hours on, 8-10 hours off
   - Light intensity: 200-300 μmol/m²/s

3. **Environmental Monitoring**
   - Record temperature 3 times daily
   - Check humidity twice daily
   - Verify light function and timer settings
   - Document all readings in growing log

### Watering Protocol

1. **Standard Watering Schedule**
   - Morning watering: 7:00-8:00 AM
   - Afternoon watering (if needed): 2:00-3:00 PM
   - Adjust based on variety and environmental conditions

2. **Watering Methods**
   - Bottom watering:
     - Place trays in shallow water (¼ inch)
     - Allow to absorb for 5-10 minutes
     - Remove and allow to drain
   - Top watering:
     - Use gentle misting or drip irrigation
     - Ensure even coverage
     - Avoid wetting foliage when possible

3. **Water Specifications**
   - Filtered water only
   - pH: 6.0-6.5
   - Temperature: Room temperature (65-70°F)
   - No fertilizers or additives for most varieties

### Daily Care Procedures

1. **Morning Inspection (7:00-8:00 AM)**
   - Check moisture levels in all trays
   - Inspect for any issues or abnormalities
   - Water as needed following protocol
   - Document observations

2. **Midday Check (12:00-1:00 PM)**
   - Quick visual inspection of all trays
   - Adjust environmental controls if needed
   - Address any emerging issues
   - Document any actions taken

3. **Evening Procedures (5:00-6:00 PM)**
   - Final daily inspection
   - Water if necessary
   - Identify trays approaching harvest
   - Prepare harvest list for following day
   - Document growth progress

4. **Rotation Protocol**
   - Rotate trays 180° every other day
   - Ensure even light distribution
   - Document rotation in growing log

### Growth Monitoring

1. **Daily Growth Assessment**
   - Measure plant height for sample trays
   - Check color and vigor
   - Assess stem thickness
   - Document growth rate

2. **Development Tracking**
   - Track development stages:
     - Germination
     - Cotyledon emergence
     - First true leaf development
     - Harvest readiness
   - Update expected harvest dates
   - Flag any trays developing abnormally

3. **Pre-Harvest Evaluation**
   - 24-48 hours before expected harvest:
     - Evaluate color and size
     - Check flavor profile
     - Assess overall quality
     - Confirm harvest timing

## Harvesting Procedures

### Harvest Preparation

1. **Harvest Planning**
   - Review orders for the day
   - Identify trays ready for harvest
   - Prepare harvest schedule
   - Organize by client and delivery route

2. **Equipment Preparation**
   - Clean and sanitize all harvest tools:
     - Scissors/harvest knives
     - Collection containers
     - Packaging materials
     - Work surfaces
   - Prepare clean harvest containers
   - Set up harvest station

3. **Pre-Harvest Quality Check**
   - Verify trays meet quality standards:
     - Proper height
     - Good color
     - No disease or damage
     - Correct maturity stage
   - Document any quality issues
   - Make final harvest decisions

### Harvesting Techniques

1. **Standard Cutting Method**
   - Hold stems gently with non-dominant hand
   - Cut just above growing medium with sharp scissors
   - Maintain consistent cutting height
   - Place cut microgreens directly into clean container
   - Avoid compressing harvested greens

2. **6-Square Tray Harvesting**
   - Harvest one variety at a time
   - Clean scissors between varieties
   - Label each variety immediately after cutting
   - Keep varieties separated throughout process

3. **Living Tray Preparation**
   - For living tray deliveries:
     - Trim edges for uniform appearance
     - Remove any discolored plants
     - Ensure growing medium is moist
     - Verify tray is clean and presentable

### Post-Harvest Handling

1. **Immediate Processing**
   - Move harvested greens to processing area within 5 minutes
   - Keep out of direct sunlight
   - Maintain cool temperature
   - Handle gently to prevent bruising

2. **Quality Sorting**
   - Remove any yellow or damaged leaves
   - Sort out any foreign material
   - Verify consistent quality throughout batch
   - Document any quality issues

3. **Cooling Protocol**
   - Place harvested greens in cooler within 15 minutes
   - Target temperature: 34-38°F (1-3°C)
   - Allow proper air circulation
   - Keep varieties separated

4. **Harvest Documentation**
   - Record in harvest log:
     - Variety
     - Harvest date and time
     - Quantity harvested
     - Days from seeding to harvest
     - Quality assessment
     - Staff member who harvested
     - Destination/client

## Packaging & Storage

### Packaging Materials

1. **Approved Packaging**
   - Compostable clamshells
   - Recyclable plastic containers
   - Kraft paper bags (for certain applications)
   - Living trays with clear covers

2. **Packaging Specifications**
   - Food-grade materials only
   - Proper ventilation for moisture control
   - Secure closure to prevent contamination
   - Clear visibility of product

3. **Packaging Preparation**
   - Inspect all packaging for damage
   - Sanitize reusable containers
   - Store packaging in clean, dry area
   - Handle with clean gloves only

### Packaging Process

1. **Standard Packaging Procedure**
   - Tare scale with empty container
   - Fill to specified weight (typically 2-4 oz)
   - Avoid compressing microgreens
   - Allow some headspace in container
   - Secure lid or closure
   - Apply label immediately

2. **Living Tray Packaging**
   - Verify tray is clean and presentable
   - Ensure growing medium is moist but not wet
   - Apply clear cover if specified
   - Secure for transport
   - Apply label to tray edge

3. **Custom Mix Creation**
   - Follow recipe specifications exactly
   - Weigh each variety separately
   - Combine gently in mixing bowl
   - Transfer to final packaging
   - Document mix composition

4. **Labeling Requirements**
   - Each package must include:
     - Variety name(s)
     - Harvest date
     - Use-by date (5-7 days from harvest)
     - ABCFarmsIL branding
     - Storage instructions
     - QR code (when applicable)

### Storage Protocol

1. **Cooler Storage Requirements**
   - Temperature: 34-38°F (1-3°C)
   - Humidity: 90-95%
   - Good air circulation
   - No ethylene-producing items in same cooler

2. **Storage Organization**
   - Arrange by delivery route
   - Implement FIFO (First In, First Out)
   - Store on clean shelves, never on floor
   - Maintain minimum 2-inch space between containers
   - Keep varieties separated to prevent flavor transfer

3. **Storage Monitoring**
   - Check cooler temperature twice daily
   - Inspect stored product daily for quality
   - Document temperature and humidity
   - Rotate stock as needed

4. **Maximum Storage Duration**
   - Cut microgreens: 5-7 days maximum
   - Living trays: 7-10 days maximum
   - Document storage duration for each batch
   - Discard any product exceeding storage limits

## Cleaning & Sanitation

### Daily Cleaning Procedures

1. **Work Surface Cleaning**
   - Frequency: Before and after each use
   - Process:
     - Remove all debris
     - Spray with approved sanitizer
     - Allow proper contact time
     - Wipe with clean cloth
     - Allow to air dry
   - Document completion

2. **Tool Sanitation**
   - Frequency: Before and after each use
   - Process:
     - Rinse to remove debris
     - Wash with soap and water
     - Sanitize with food-grade sanitizer
     - Air dry on clean rack
     - Store in designated area
   - Document completion

3. **Floor Cleaning**
   - Frequency: End of each day
   - Process:
     - Sweep all areas
     - Mop with approved floor cleaner
     - Allow to dry completely
     - Check for any missed areas
   - Document completion

### Weekly Deep Cleaning

1. **Growing Rack Sanitation**
   - Frequency: Weekly, rotating schedule
   - Process:
     - Remove all trays from section
     - Wipe down all surfaces
     - Spray with hydrogen peroxide solution
     - Wipe with clean cloth
     - Allow to dry before replacing trays
   - Document completion

2. **Water System Cleaning**
   - Frequency: Weekly
   - Process:
     - Flush all lines with hydrogen peroxide solution
     - Clean all nozzles and filters
     - Rinse thoroughly
     - Test water quality after cleaning
   - Document completion

3. **Cooler Cleaning**
   - Frequency: Weekly
   - Process:
     - Remove all products
     - Wipe all surfaces with sanitizer
     - Clean door seals
     - Check and clean drain
     - Allow to return to temperature before restocking
   - Document completion

### Tray Cleaning Protocol

1. **Used Tray Processing**
   - Remove all plant material and growing medium
   - Rinse with water to remove debris
   - Soak in sanitizing solution (1:10 bleach solution or approved alternative)
   - Scrub if necessary to remove residue
   - Rinse thoroughly with clean water
   - Air dry completely before reuse
   - Inspect for damage before returning to inventory
   - Document cleaning process

2. **Sanitizing Solutions**
   - Standard solution: 1:10 bleach solution (1 part bleach to 10 parts water)
   - Alternative solution: Hydrogen peroxide (3%)
   - Vinegar solution (for certain applications): 1:3 vinegar to water
   - Prepare fresh solutions daily
   - Test concentration with test strips
   - Document solution preparation

3. **Tray Storage**
   - Store only completely dry trays
   - Stack no more than 20 trays high
   - Store in clean, designated area
   - Protect from dust and contamination
   - Implement FIFO rotation

### Pest Management Cleaning

1. **Preventative Measures**
   - Keep all areas free of debris
   - Seal any entry points
   - Maintain proper drainage
   - Store seeds and growing media in sealed containers
   - Regular inspection of facility perimeter

2. **Monitoring Protocol**
   - Weekly inspection of all areas
   - Check sticky traps
   - Document any signs of pests
   - Identify entry points or attractants

3. **Response Procedures**
   - Isolate affected areas
   - Increase cleaning frequency
   - Use approved organic pest control methods
   - Document all actions taken
   - Follow up with preventative measures

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Germination Problems

1. **Low Germination Rate**
   - Possible causes:
     - Old or poor quality seeds
     - Improper temperature
     - Incorrect moisture levels
     - Contaminated growing medium
   - Solutions:
     - Test seed viability
     - Verify environmental conditions
     - Adjust watering protocol
     - Replace growing medium
     - Document interventions and results

2. **Uneven Germination**
   - Possible causes:
     - Uneven seeding density
     - Inconsistent moisture
     - Temperature variations
     - Uneven pressure (for weighted trays)
   - Solutions:
     - Improve seeding technique
     - Ensure even watering
     - Check for temperature gradients
     - Adjust weighting method
     - Document interventions and results

3. **Mold During Germination**
   - Possible causes:
     - Excessive moisture
     - Poor air circulation
     - Contaminated seeds or medium
     - Too high humidity
   - Solutions:
     - Reduce watering
     - Improve air flow
     - Consider seed sanitization
     - Lower humidity
     - Document interventions and results

### Growing Phase Issues

1. **Leggy Growth (Etiolation)**
   - Possible causes:
     - Insufficient light
     - Overcrowding
     - Too high temperature
   - Solutions:
     - Adjust light height or intensity
     - Reduce seeding density
     - Lower temperature
     - Document interventions and results

2. **Yellowing Leaves**
   - Possible causes:
     - Overwatering
     - Nutrient issues
     - Disease
     - End of life cycle
   - Solutions:
     - Adjust watering schedule
     - Check growing medium quality
     - Isolate if disease suspected
     - Harvest earlier if appropriate
