## **🧾 Purpose**

To define the standard procedure for receiving, sanitizing, and redeploying **empty, returned trays and bins** from chef clients or pilot hosts.  
This process ensures that all packaging is **washed, sanitized, and air-dried** in compliance with USDA/Illinois Extension food-safety expectations while preserving ABCFARMSIL’s closed-loop, low-water operation model.

---

## **⚙️ Equipment / Infrastructure**

* **40-gallon food-grade return tote** with lid (labeled “Return Sanitation Tote – No Rinse Cycle”).  
* **Approved food-safe sanitizer** (e.g., *Star San* 1 oz/5 gal water @ pH 3.5 or Dr. Bronner’s solution per label\*).  
* **Sanitation rack / drying rack** – NSF stainless or polymer.  
* **Non-contact thermometer (optional)** – confirm ambient drying conditions \> 60 °F.  
* **Sanitation log sheet (this form)**.

---

## **🧼 Procedure**

1. **Receive Returns**  
   * Confirm all trays are **empty of food, soil, and debris**.  
   * Log date/time of receipt and count of trays.  
2. **Load Tote**  
   * Fill tote with sanitizer solution (prepared fresh daily).  
   * Verify pH/concentration with test strip if applicable.  
3. **Sanitize**  
   * Fully submerge or spray trays for **1 minute minimum contact time**.  
   * Do **not rinse** after sanitizer exposure (no-rinse protocol).  
4. **Drying Stage**  
   * Place trays on designated drying rack in the **clean zone**.  
   * Allow to **air-dry completely** (minimum 30 min; no towel drying).  
5. **Re-Label / QR Assignment**  
   * Once dry, assign new QR label or update tray ID in MRV log.  
   * Move to “Clean Inventory” storage.  
6. **Recordkeeping**  
   * Complete the log below.  
   * Store this sheet in *Sanitation Binder → Section 1C (Return Cycle)*.

## **✅ Safety & Compliance Notes**

* Always wear gloves and eye protection when handling sanitizer.  
* Replace solution if visibly soiled or after 4 hours of use.  
* Log sheet must be signed by rack operator and reviewed weekly by system steward.  
* Do not mix sanitizer brands or reuse expired solution.
