## How Warm Intros Actually Get Created

Nobody starts with warm intros. They CREATE them. Here's how:

### Scenario A: The Cold Call That Becomes Warm
```
Day 1: Call restaurant → Voicemail
Day 3: Call again → "Not interested"  
Day 7: Email with sample photos → "Send them over"
Day 10: Deliver free tray → Chef tries it
Day 14: Follow-up call → "This is good, let's talk pricing"
Day 30: Chef introduces you to his restaurant owner friend
Day 45: THAT friend becomes your second customer
```

**Result:** You now have a warm intro. Created by YOU through cold outreach.

### Scenario B: The "I Need Warm Intros" Approach
```
Day 1: Decide you need warm intros
Day 7: Attend networking event, don't know anyone
Day 14: Attend another event, still no connections
Day 21: Ask friends if they know restaurant owners (they don't)
Day 30: Realize you've wasted a month waiting
Day 45: Still no customers, still no warm intros
```

**Result:** Zero progress, still waiting for permission to start.

## The "Members Only" Reality

You're right that some places are members-only. Let's break that down:

### Private Clubs & Exclusive Venues
- YES, they're exclusive
- YES, they have members for a reason
- BUT: They still buy food. Someone sells to them.
- That someone started by calling them.

### The Vendor Exception
- Restaurants and clubs have vendor relationships
- Vendors don't need to be members
- They need to provide value
- You're a vendor. You don't need a membership. You need a product.

### The Real Question
If "members only" places are your target market, how will you EVER get introduced if you don't start the relationship yourself?

The person who introduces you... how did THEY get introduced to the restaurant? THEY probably started as a cold vendor too.
