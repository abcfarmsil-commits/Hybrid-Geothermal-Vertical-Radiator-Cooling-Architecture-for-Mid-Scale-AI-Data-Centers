## Introduction

### Purpose and Scope
This delivery logistics plan outlines the comprehensive strategy for transporting ABCFarmsIL's microgreens from production facility to clients. The plan covers all aspects of the delivery process including route planning, vehicle requirements, packaging, delivery protocols, quality control, and performance measurement. It is designed to ensure that products reach clients in optimal condition while maximizing operational efficiency and sustainability.

### Strategic Importance
Delivery logistics represents a critical component of ABCFarmsIL's value proposition. Our ability to deliver ultra-fresh microgreens within hours of harvest directly impacts product quality, client satisfaction, and business profitability. The delivery system is not merely a transportation function but a key differentiator that enables our "harvest freshness, delivered sustainably" promise.

### Core Delivery Principles
1. **Freshness Preservation:** Maintain optimal product conditions throughout the delivery process
2. **Timeliness:** Deliver within promised time windows to accommodate client operations
3. **Efficiency:** Optimize routes and resources to minimize costs and environmental impact
4. **Professionalism:** Represent the ABCFarmsIL brand through every client interaction
5. **Sustainability:** Minimize environmental footprint through efficient routing and packaging
6. **Adaptability:** Maintain flexibility to accommodate special requests and changing conditions
7. **Continuous Improvement:** Regularly analyze performance data to enhance delivery operations

## Delivery Service Overview

### Service Territory
- **Primary Service Area:** Chicago city limits
- **Secondary Service Area:** Inner suburbs within 15 miles of production facility
- **Tertiary Service Area (Special Arrangements):** Outer suburbs within 25 miles
- **Service Area Map:** See Appendix A for detailed service area map with zones

### Delivery Schedule
- **Standard Delivery Days:** Monday through Friday
- **Delivery Hours:** 7:00 AM - 2:00 PM
- **Delivery Windows:**
  - Early Morning (7:00 AM - 9:00 AM): Primarily for restaurants preparing for lunch service
  - Mid-Morning (9:00 AM - 11:00 AM): Hotels, country clubs, and specialty food businesses
  - Midday (11:00 AM - 2:00 PM): Restaurants preparing for dinner service
- **Special Arrangements:**
  - Saturday deliveries available with 48-hour notice (additional fee may apply)
  - Off-hours deliveries by special arrangement (additional fee may apply)

### Service Levels
- **Standard Delivery:** Next-day delivery for orders placed by 12:00 PM
- **Premium Delivery:** Same-day delivery for orders placed by 8:00 AM (limited availability, additional fee)
- **Scheduled Recurring Delivery:** Standing orders on set days/times
- **Custom Delivery Programs:** Tailored to specific client needs (e.g., multiple deliveries per week, specific time requirements)

### Delivery Fees
- **Zone-Based Pricing:**
  - Zone 1 (0-5 miles): Included with minimum order of $50
  - Zone 2 (5-10 miles): Included with minimum order of $75
  - Zone 3 (10-15 miles): Included with minimum order of $100
  - Zone 4 (15-25 miles): $15 delivery fee or included with minimum order of $150
- **Order Size Incentives:**
  - Orders over $200: Priority scheduling
  - Orders over $300: Complimentary delivery regardless of zone
- **Special Service Fees:**
  - Same-day delivery: $25 additional fee
  - Saturday delivery: $15 additional fee
  - Off-hours delivery: $25 additional fee

### Seasonal Adjustments
- **Winter Operations (December-February):**
  - Extended delivery windows to account for weather delays
  - Additional insulation for temperature protection
  - Weather-based route adjustments
- **Summer Operations (June-August):**
  - Earlier delivery start times (6:00 AM) during heat waves
  - Additional cooling measures
  - More frequent temperature monitoring

## Route Planning & Optimization

### Route Planning Process

#### Daily Planning Cycle
- **Day Before Delivery (by 2:00 PM):**
  - Consolidate all orders for next-day delivery
  - Group orders by geographic zone
  - Preliminary route mapping
  - Vehicle assignment
  - Driver notification

- **Morning of Delivery (5:30 AM):**
  - Finalize routes with any last-minute orders
  - Check weather and traffic conditions
  - Make any necessary adjustments
  - Brief drivers on routes and special instructions
  - Distribute final route sheets and loading plans

- **Post-Delivery (End of Day):**
  - Collect delivery feedback
  - Document any route issues
  - Update client delivery notes
  - Analyze route efficiency
  - Identify improvement opportunities

#### Route Optimization Factors
- **Geographic Clustering:**
  - Group deliveries by neighborhood/district
  - Minimize distance between stops
  - Consider natural boundaries (rivers, highways)
  - Account for one-way streets and traffic patterns

- **Time Window Constraints:**
  - Prioritize time-sensitive deliveries
  - Group deliveries with similar time windows
  - Allow buffer time between stops
  - Account for typical unloading time at each location

- **Traffic Considerations:**
  - Avoid peak traffic hours when possible
  - Use real-time traffic data for adjustments
  - Plan alternative routes for high-traffic areas
  - Account for construction and road closures

- **Client-Specific Requirements:**
  - Special delivery entrance locations
  - Restricted delivery hours
  - Security check-in procedures
  - Typical wait times at location

### Optimization Tools & Techniques

#### Routing Software
- **Primary Tool:** Route4Me or similar routing optimization software
- **Key Features:**
  - Multi-stop route optimization
  - Time window constraints
  - Real-time traffic integration
  - Mobile app for drivers
  - Delivery confirmation
  - Performance analytics

- **Implementation Process:**
  - Import client database with locations and preferences
  - Set up standard routes for recurring deliveries
  - Train staff on software usage
  - Integrate with order management system
  - Regular performance review and refinement

#### Manual Optimization Techniques
- **Zone-Based Planning:**
  - Divide service area into delivery zones
  - Assign specific days/times to each zone
  - Encourage clients to align with zone schedule
  - Offer incentives for flexible delivery windows

- **Standing Route Development:**
  - Create standard routes for recurring clients
  - Optimize these routes for efficiency
  - Schedule new clients along existing routes when possible
  - Periodically review and adjust standing routes

- **Driver Knowledge Integration:**
  - Incorporate driver feedback on routes
  - Document shortcuts and traffic patterns
  - Note building access information
  - Record parking availability and restrictions

### Route Density & Efficiency

#### Minimum Delivery Density
- **Target Metrics:**
  - Minimum 3 deliveries per hour
  - Maximum 15 minutes between stops
  - Minimum $100 revenue per hour of delivery time
  - Maximum 25% of route time spent driving between stops

- **Density Improvement Strategies:**
  - Offer delivery day discounts to encourage clustering
  - Implement zone-based delivery days
  - Provide incentives for clients to accept wider delivery windows
  - Develop minimum order requirements by distance

#### Efficiency Optimization
- **Vehicle Loading:**
  - Load in reverse order of delivery
  - Group orders by delivery sequence
  - Minimize rearrangement during route
  - Optimize space utilization

- **Stop Sequencing:**
  - Prioritize time-sensitive deliveries
  - Account for business opening hours
  - Consider typical traffic patterns
  - Plan for known access restrictions

- **Continuous Improvement:**
  - Weekly route analysis
  - Driver feedback sessions
  - Client delivery preference updates
  - Seasonal route adjustments

## Vehicle & Equipment Requirements

### Delivery Vehicle Specifications

#### Primary Delivery Vehicle
- **Vehicle Type:** Fuel-efficient cargo van (e.g., Ford Transit Connect or similar)
- **Quantity:** 1 vehicle initially, scaling to 3 by year 3
- **Key Requirements:**
  - Cargo capacity: Minimum 100 cubic feet
  - Payload capacity: Minimum 1,500 pounds
  - Fuel efficiency: Minimum 20 MPG combined
  - Maneuverability: Suitable for urban parking and navigation
  - Access: Rear and side cargo doors
  - Age: Not older than 5 years

- **Environmental Considerations:**
  - Transition plan to hybrid or electric vehicles by year 3
  - Regular maintenance for optimal fuel efficiency
  - Route optimization to minimize mileage
  - Idle reduction policy

#### Backup Delivery Options
- **Short-Term Backup:**
  - Rental agreement with local vehicle rental company
  - Staff personal vehicle usage policy (with compensation structure)
  - On-demand delivery service partnership

- **Long-Term Contingency:**
  - Delivery service partnership for overflow or emergency situations
  - Reciprocal arrangement with complementary local food businesses
  - Client pickup option from production facility

### Climate Control & Monitoring

#### Temperature Control System
- **Cooling Requirements:**
  - Maintain 34-38°F (1-3°C) during transport
  - Pre-cool cargo area before loading
  - Insulated cargo area
  - Capability to maintain temperature for minimum 4 hours

- **Equipment Options:**
  - Phase 1: Commercial-grade insulated containers with ice packs
  - Phase 2: Small-scale refrigeration unit installation
  - Phase 3: Purpose-built refrigerated van

- **Monitoring System:**
  - Digital temperature loggers in cargo area
  - Real-time temperature monitoring capability
  - Alert system for temperature excursions
  - Temperature log for quality assurance

#### Humidity Control
- **Requirements:**
  - Maintain 90-95% humidity for cut microgreens
  - Prevent excess moisture on packaging
  - Avoid condensation issues

- **Control Methods:**
  - Humidity-controlled packaging
  - Moisture-retaining materials
  - Proper air circulation
  - Regular monitoring

## Packaging & Loading Procedures

### Pre-Delivery Preparation

#### Order Assembly
- **Timing:** 30-60 minutes before scheduled departure
- **Process:**
  1. Generate pick list from order management system
  2. Harvest/retrieve products from cooler
  3. Quality check each item
  4. Assemble order components
  5. Verify complete order accuracy
  6. Apply order label with client information
  7. Stage for loading in delivery sequence

- **Quality Verification:**
  - Visual inspection of all products
  - Temperature check of refrigerated items
  - Verification against order specifications
  - Final appearance and packaging check
  - Documentation of any quality issues

#### Loading Sequence
- **Preparation:**
  - Pre-cool delivery containers
  - Prepare vehicle cargo area
  - Organize loading area by route
  - Verify delivery sequence

- **Loading Process:**
  1. Load in reverse order of delivery
  2. Secure items to prevent shifting
  3. Ensure proper temperature maintenance
  4. Verify all orders loaded
  5. Complete loading checklist
  6. Lock and secure vehicle

- **Documentation:**
  - Loading checklist completion
  - Cargo temperature verification
  - Departure time recording
  - Driver acknowledgment of load
  - Any special handling notes

### Packaging Standards

#### Primary Packaging
- **Cut Microgreens:**

  - Clear top for product visibility
  - Proper ventilation to maintain freshness
  - Secure closure to prevent spilling
  - Complete labeling with variety, harvest date, use-by date

- **Living Trays:**
  - Original growing container with stabilization
  - Clear protective cover when appropriate
  - Moisture-retention system for extended shelf life
  - Handling instructions attached
  - Complete labeling with variety, harvest date, care instructions

- **Custom Mixes:**
  - Appropriate size container based on quantity
  - Clear component identification
  - Mix-specific usage recommendations
  - Complete labeling with components, harvest date, use-by date

#### Transport Packaging
- **Standard Orders:**
  - Insulated delivery containers
  - Order separation dividers
  - Temperature-appropriate cooling elements
  - Moisture control materials
  - Order identification and sequence labeling

- **Large Orders:**
  - Stackable container systems
  - Secure stacking methods
  - Reinforced handles for heavy loads
  - Clear "This Side Up" markings
  - Special handling instructions when needed

- **Fragile Items:**
  - Additional protective packaging
  - "Handle With Care" labeling
  - Separate placement in vehicle
  - Special loading instructions
  - Delivery notification for careful handling

### Product Stability & Protection

#### Temperature Maintenance
- **Cold Chain Management:**
  - Pre-cooled products (34-38°F)
  - Pre-cooled transport containers
  - Appropriate cooling elements based on route duration
  - Temperature monitoring during transport
  - Minimized exposure to ambient temperatures

- **Seasonal Adjustments:**
  - Additional cooling elements during summer
  - Insulation modifications based on season
  - Adjusted loading procedures during extreme weather
  - Route timing adjustments to avoid temperature extremes

#### Physical Protection
- **Movement Prevention:**
  - Non-slip mats in vehicle
  - Secure strapping systems
  - Compartmentalized storage
  - Proper weight distribution
  - Driver training on smooth driving techniques

- **Stacking Guidelines:**
  - Maximum stack heights
  - Proper orientation ("This Side Up")
  - Weight distribution considerations
  - Heavier items on bottom
  - Fragile items on top or separately secured

## Quality Control During Transport

### Temperature Management

#### Temperature Monitoring System
- **Equipment:**
  - Digital temperature loggers in transport containers
  - Real-time monitoring capability via Bluetooth/cellular
  - Temperature display visible to driver
  - Alert system for out-of-range temperatures

- **Monitoring Protocol:**
  - Continuous monitoring during transport
  - Recording at minimum 15-minute intervals
  - Alert thresholds: Below 33°F or above 40°F
  - Documentation of temperature log with delivery records

- **Response Procedures:**
  - Minor deviation (38-40°F): Monitor closely, adjust cooling
  - Moderate deviation (40-45°F): Expedite remaining deliveries, notify clients
  - Severe deviation (>45°F): Quality check products, potential return to facility, client notification

#### Seasonal Adaptations
- **Summer Protocols (June-August):**
  - Additional cooling elements
  - Reduced time between stops
  - Earlier delivery schedule
  - Additional insulation
  - More frequent temperature checks

- **Winter Protocols (December-February):**
  - Protection from freezing
  - Heated delivery containers when necessary
  - Reduced exposure to elements
  - Adjusted route planning for weather
  - Vehicle warming procedures
